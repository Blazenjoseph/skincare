Stability & Storage

Shelf Life of
Skincare Products

Cleansers
1 – 2 Years

Foaming and gel cleansers are generally stable due to their surfactant base and preservative system. Opened: use within 12 months. Store at room temperature, away from steam and moisture.

Toners
1 – 2 Years

Alcohol-based toners tend to last longer than hydrating, ferment-rich toners. Keep sealed and refrigerate after opening for best results with active-laden formulas.

Vitamin C Serums
3 – 12 Months

Highly unstable — L-ascorbic acid oxidizes rapidly upon exposure to light and air. Use within 3 months of opening. Refrigerate and use opaque, airless packaging whenever possible.

Retinol Serums
6 – 12 Months

Retinol degrades with light and oxygen exposure. Store in a cool, dark location. Airless pumps preserve potency significantly longer than open jars.

Hyaluronic Acid Serum
1 – 2 Years

One of the most stable actives. HA serums retain efficacy well, though preservatives may degrade over time. Keep at stable room temperature (15–25°C).

Moisturizers
1 – 3 Years

Oil-heavy creams can turn rancid faster. Water-based lotions with good preservative systems last 2+ years. Avoid dipping fingers — use spatulas to prevent contamination.

Sunscreen
2 – 3 Years

Chemical UV filters degrade with prolonged heat and light exposure. Never leave in a hot car. Expired sunscreen loses its SPF protection — discard past the date.

Face Masks
1 – 2 Years

Sheet masks are single-use and sealed — use promptly once opened. Clay masks in jars should be used within 6–12 months of opening to maintain ingredient efficacy.

Lip Balm
1 – 2 Years

Natural butter and oil-based balms are susceptible to rancidity. Sealed balms last up to 2 years but should be discarded if the scent or texture changes markedly.

Exfoliants (AHA/BHA)
1 – 2 Years

Acid exfoliants are relatively stable if kept sealed and away from light. pH can shift over time, reducing efficacy. Refrigerating after opening helps maintain acid strength.

🌡️

Ideal Temperature
Most products prefer 15–25°C. Avoid bathrooms where steam accelerates degradation.

🌑

Darkness is Key
UV light degrades actives like Vitamin C, retinol and certain preservatives. Use opaque packaging.

🧪

Check the PAO Symbol
The open-jar symbol shows months after opening (e.g., 12M = use within 12 months once opened).

⚠️

Signs of Expiry
Colour change, off smell, separation, thickening or pH shift all signal degradation — discard promptly.

🔴 Acidic (pH 3.5–4.5)

Typical of exfoliating products with AHAs/BHAs. Effective for cell turnover but may cause sensitivity in some skin types.

🟡 Slightly Acidic (pH 4.5–5.5)

Close to skin's natural pH (~5.5). Ideal for serums, toners, and most treatment products. Well-tolerated by most skin types.

🟢 Balanced (pH 5.5–6.5)

Perfect for moisturizers and daily-use products. Maintains skin barrier integrity without disruption.

🔵 Neutral (pH 6.5–7.5)

Common in cleansers. While gentle, prolonged use of high-pH cleansers may temporarily disrupt the acid mantle.
